Mini Challenges - Dec 16th 2013

Terminal

In this challenge you will:

  • connect to a remote server,
  • make a change to a webpage hosted there,
  • and confirm the change through a web browser.

Follow these steps:

  1. Visit this link in a new browser window:
    http://rsu3.hacktivate.org/hackers.html
  2. Open the terminal.
  3. Connect to remote webserver:
    ssh hacker@hacktivate.org
  4. Move to directory which hosts the webpage for our hacker club:
    cd /var/www/rsu3
  5. Open an editor to change the file:
    edit hackers.html
  6. Make a change to the page. Add your hacker details. Have fun with it. We're all friends here.

There is a wealth of material online, which you can use to gain incredible skills. Choose your content wisely. Here are some videos to spark your imagination and introduce some new tools.

    Linus Torvalds is from Finland. He created the free, open-source Linux kernel. Watch this 3 minute video where Torvalds is asked about teaching kids to program. To complete this challenge, ask someone else in this room who watched the video what he or she thought of Torvalds' opinion.

Allies

You are surrounded by potential allies

A wise woman once said this, and it couldn't be more true! You have the ability to reach out and connect with people who will support your ideas & interests. All you have to do is seek to make the connection and it will happen, one way or another.

In this challenge you will:

  1. Consider a goal you want to achieve. It could be learning a new skill, or simply understanding some new concept.
  2. Describe a person who could help you toward this goal. List a name if you know it, or list attributes if you don't have a particular person in mind. For example:
    1. A professor
    2. who teaches physics
    3. and enjoys programming
    Or maybe something a little different:
    1. A programmer
    2. who makes minecraft
    3. and could teach me how to do it
  3. Tell us about it by choosing one of these options:
    • Post about it on the RSU 3 hacker club forum.
    • Email us @ hello@hacktivate.org.
